PALM BAY MEDICAL CENTER SETTLES MEDICARE FRAUD ALLEGATIONS FOR $3.1 MILLION
By: J. Hartley, Florida Health News Staff Reporter
Published: March 14, 2019

PALM BAY, FL — Palm Bay Medical Center, a multi-specialty outpatient clinic
in Brevard County, has agreed to pay $3.1 million to resolve allegations that
it systematically overbilled Medicare for office visits between 2014 and 2018.

The settlement, reached with the U.S. Department of Justice and announced
Thursday, resolves a qui tam lawsuit filed by a former billing specialist at
the clinic. The whistleblower alleged that Palm Bay directed its providers to
bill all patient encounters at the highest complexity code (CPT 99215)
regardless of the actual services rendered.

"Medicare beneficiaries deserve accurate billing that reflects the care they
actually receive," said the Acting U.S. Attorney in a statement. "This
settlement sends a clear message that systematic upcoding will be detected
and will result in significant financial penalties."

Palm Bay Medical admitted no wrongdoing as part of the civil settlement. The
clinic entered into a five-year Corporate Integrity Agreement with the Office
of Inspector General.

Dr. Victor Mendez, who served as Clinical Director of Palm Bay's Family
Medicine division from 2015 to 2018, was not named as an individual defendant
in the settlement. A Palm Bay spokesperson said Dr. Mendez departed the clinic
in September 2018 "to pursue other opportunities."

The whistleblower, whose identity was kept confidential per the terms of the
settlement, received approximately $527,000 — 17% of the government's recovery.
