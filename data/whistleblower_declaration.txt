WHISTLEBLOWER DECLARATION AND DISCLOSURE STATEMENT
In the Matter of: United States ex rel. Okonkwo v. Sunrise Family Medicine Group
Relator: Sarah Okonkwo, NP
Prepared for Submission Under 31 U.S.C. § 3730(b)(2)
================================================================================

I, Sarah Okonkwo, a Nurse Practitioner licensed in the State of Florida
(License No. NP-FL-88421), hereby declare under penalty of perjury that the
following is true and correct to the best of my knowledge and belief:

SECTION 1 — MY BACKGROUND

1.1  I was employed at Sunrise Family Medicine Group ("Sunrise" or "the Clinic")
     from March 4, 2019 through my termination on November 14, 2023.
     My employee ID was EMP-0047. My NPI is 8899001122.

1.2  My role was Nurse Practitioner — I saw approximately 18-22 patients per
     day. I had full access to the EHR system (AthenaHealth) and reviewed my
     own billing submissions daily.

1.3  I have no financial relationship with any competitor of Sunrise Family
     Medicine Group. I am disclosing this information because I believe a
     federal crime is being committed using Medicare funds.

SECTION 2 — THE FRAUDULENT SCHEME

2.1  Beginning approximately March 2020, Dr. Victor Mendez, Medical Director
     and majority owner of Sunrise Family Medicine Group, issued a verbal and
     then written directive requiring all providers to bill all patient
     encounters at CPT code 99215 (Level 5, High Complexity E&M) regardless
     of the actual complexity of the visit.

2.2  I personally witnessed this directive issued at a staff meeting on
     approximately March 10, 2020. A follow-up email was sent to all staff
     on March 12, 2020 (preserved and attached as Exhibit A).

2.3  The Medicare billing rules for CPT 99215 require that the medical
     decision making involve: (a) multiple diagnoses or management options,
     (b) extensive amount and/or complexity of data reviewed, AND (c) high
     risk of complications. The vast majority of visits at Sunrise did not
     meet these criteria.

2.4  I reviewed a sample of 40 of my own claims from December 2020. Of those:
     — 12 were routine wellness visits (ICD-10: Z00.00) which cannot lawfully
       be billed as 99215
     — 9 were upper respiratory infections with straightforward management
     — 8 were stable chronic disease follow-ups with no change in treatment
     — Only 9 had legitimate clinical complexity

2.5  I formally reported my concerns to Dr. Mendez in writing on January 14,
     2021 (attached as Exhibit B). He responded by threatening my employment
     and my March 2021 contract renewal (attached as Exhibit C).

2.6  I was terminated on November 14, 2023. I believe my termination was
     directly related to my continued refusal to sign off on fraudulently
     upcoded claims, particularly the wart removal claim (CLM101834) that
     Rania Hassan resubmitted under my NPI without my authorization.

SECTION 3 — THE UNBUNDLING SCHEME

3.1  In addition to upcoding, beginning approximately March 2021, Office
     Manager Rania Hassan issued a written directive (attached as Exhibit D)
     requiring providers to add lab and diagnostic codes (81003, 85025, 80053,
     93000) to virtually all Medicare patient visits using Modifier -59.

3.2  Modifier -59 is used to indicate a service is distinct from another
     service billed on the same day. The Clinic used it routinely to
     separately bill for tests that were ordered not for a clinical indication
     but as a means to increase reimbursement.

3.3  On numerous occasions, I observed urinalysis (81003) being billed for
     patients who had no urinary complaints and for whom I had not ordered
     the test. The orders were added by billing staff.

SECTION 4 — EVIDENCE TAMPERING

4.1  I observed on multiple occasions that patient charts were edited AFTER
     the claim had been submitted to and paid by Medicare. These edits
     consisted of adding language about "complex chronic disease management"
     and "high medical decision making" that was not in the original note.

4.2  I was able to recover the original note text for 12 claims using the
     EHR's version history feature before my access was revoked. These
     before-and-after versions are attached as Exhibit E (ehr_audit_log.csv).

4.3  I observed that at least some edits were made by user "rmhassan" —
     Rania Hassan, the Office Manager — who has no clinical training and
     is not a licensed healthcare provider.

SECTION 5 — PRIOR KNOWLEDGE OF DEFENDANT

5.1  I am aware from a 2018 Florida Health News article (attached as Exhibit F)
     that Dr. Victor Mendez was previously employed at Palm Bay Medical Center
     in Brevard County, Florida. Palm Bay Medical settled a Medicare fraud
     case for $3.1 million in early 2019, six months after Dr. Mendez
     departed. The settlement involved the same pattern of systematic
     upcoding of E&M services.

5.2  This prior history is relevant to the Scienter element — Dr. Mendez
     was present during a prior fraud investigation involving identical
     billing patterns. He cannot credibly claim ignorance of the rules.

SECTION 6 — ESTIMATED GOVERNMENT LOSS

6.1  Based on my review of billing data I preserved prior to termination,
     Sunrise Family Medicine Group submitted approximately 12,847 Medicare
     claims between January 2021 and December 2023.

6.2  Approximately 91% of these claims (approximately 11,690 claims) were
     billed at CPT 99215. Based on the national Family Medicine benchmark
     (14.1% of visits should be 99215), approximately 9,906 of these were
     fraudulently upcoded.

6.3  The differential between CPT 99215 ($203.44) and CPT 99213 ($97.02)
     is $106.42. After Medicare's 80% payment rate, the overpayment per
     claim is approximately $85.14.

6.4  Estimated base overpayment: 9,906 × $85.14 = $843,516
     Treble damages under 31 U.S.C. § 3729: $843,516 × 3 = $2,530,548
     Civil penalties: $13,946 to $27,894 × 9,906 claims = $138.1M to $276.3M

6.5  This does not include the unbundled lab and diagnostic codes, which I
     estimate represent an additional $350,000–$600,000 in false claims.
